Building Better Websites

There are an almost infinite amount of blog posts, presentations and books that comment on how you should and shouldn't build websites. Unsurprisingly perhaps, they appear to disagree on a lot. They spawn lots and lots of commentary and argument and debate. Entire "camps" and "factions" seem to form around particular arguments or philosophies. There are lots of landmines because of language: "You don't care about X?" makes people feel defensive. So, what exactly is the better website and how do you build it? In this post, I'll tackle that question as honestly as I can.

Ok, so - the secret to building better websites.. I'm going to let you in on a little secret, are you ready for it? Drumroll please...

It depends.

"It depends" doesn't sound like much of an answer on its own - it's not exactly helpful, I realize. The first, and probably most obvious thing worth admitting In 2005, most operating systems shipped with some simple text editor for rich text which were capable of saving things in .txt or .rtf formats. Microsoft Word was pretty much the defacto standard for "real" word processing. But then, suddenly, someone said "what if you could do that in the browser"? A full blown word processor in the browser was a novel idea, but it definitely wasn't a 'page' anymore or was there an immediately obvious way to accomplish it via form fields.
